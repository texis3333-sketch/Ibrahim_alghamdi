History of Diriyah

Diriyah was once the capital of the first Saudi state. It grew around the fertile Wadi Hanifah valley and became a centre of trade, religion, and governance in the heart of the Arabian Peninsula.

Timeline of Diriyah

Year Event
15th century Diriyah founded along Wadi Hanifah.
1744 Beginning of the First Saudi State, with Diriyah as its capital.
1818 Diriyah falls after conflict with the Ottoman Empire.
20th century Restoration efforts begin to protect the historic mud-brick structures.
2010 Diriyah listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Learn more at UNESCO World Heritage Centre
